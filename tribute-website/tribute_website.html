Lata Mangeshkar

1929-2022

Nightingale of India

Mangeshkar, born on September 28, 1929, is an iconic Indian playback singer known as the "Nightingale of India." She comes from a musical family and started her career in Marathi and Hindi films. Her soulful voice and versatility made her the most sought-after singer in the Indian film industry, and she has worked with renowned music directors. Lata's contributions to Indian music are unparalleled, and her songs continue to touch hearts and uplift spirits. She is also known for her philanthropy and humble nature. Lata Mangeshkar's legacy as a living legend will forever remain in the hearts of music enthusiasts.

Award won by Lata Mangeshkar

  • Bharat Ratna: In 2001, Lata Mangeshkar was honored with India's highest civilian award, Bharat Ratna, for her exceptional contributions to the field of music.
  • Dadasaheb Phalke Award: In 1989, she became the first playback singer to receive the prestigious Dadasaheb Phalke Award, recognizing her outstanding contributions to Indian cinema.
  • Padma Bhushan: Lata Mangeshkar was honored with the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 1969, for her distinguished service to the field of music.
  • Padma Vibhushan: In 1999, she received the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India, in recognition of her exceptional achievements and contributions to the arts.
  • Filmfare Awards: Lata Mangeshkar holds the record for the most Filmfare Awards for Best Female Playback Singer, having won the award a staggering seven times. She has also received several other Filmfare Awards throughout her career.
